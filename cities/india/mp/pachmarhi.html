Forest days,
done without a rush.

Arrive, rest, then give Pachmarhi two full days: waterfalls and forest on one day; caves, valleys and town on the next. Keep a third sightseeing day only for a trek or safari.

DO THIS FIRST

Permit, vehicle
and weather check.

Book or ask early

Before breakfast, confirm that day's forest permit and authorised Gypsy/vehicle at the local forest counter or through the official permit system. Weekend queues can be longer.

Open official forest portal ↗

Wednesday warning

Forest sightseeing is commonly restricted from Wednesday afternoon for maintenance. Do town sights, arrival or market on Wednesday, and confirm at the gate.

Wear the right shoes

Bee Fall, Apsara Vihar, Reechgarh, caves and Chauragarh involve steps, uneven rocks or short trails. Carry water; do not rely on slippers.

Monsoon rule

Beautiful season, but access, visibility and water levels can change quickly. Never enter a closed trail or cross a flowing stream.

The waterfall circuit

Keep this day for the forest-side places. Start at the permit counter, follow the authorised route and finish at Dhoopgarh sunset only if weather is clear.

Permit and vehicle confirmation

Confirm the day’s open points, exit time and vehicle arrangement before leaving. Do not pay an unofficial person without a receipt or clear booking detail.

Go early for cooler steps and less crowd. It involves a downhill/uphill walk; allow time for the climb back.

FOR FIT TRAVELLERS

Chauragarh trek

A temple climb with many steps. Start early, carry water and make it the main event of the day—especially for families or first-time trekkers.
