WHEN TO VISIT MP

Choose the trip
by the month.

MP changes with heat, rains, festivals and forest seasons. Pick the experience you want, then verify current weather, access and official bookings close to travel date.

OCTOBER–FEBRUARY

Comfortable exploring.

Best all-round season for heritage walks, temples, city food routes and family travel. Start early at popular monuments and expect holiday/festival crowd in busy places.

GwaliorKhajurahoOrchhaUjjainBhopal
Choose this if: it is your first MP trip, you are travelling with family, or you want maximum outdoor sightseeing comfort.
MARCH–JUNE

Heat-smart travel.

Keep city sightseeing early morning and evening. Pachmarhi is a better hill escape than the hotter plains. Wildlife travellers should book only confirmed permits and carry strong heat protection.

PachmarhiKanhaBandhavgarhPench
Do not: fill noon with forts, markets and long road transfers. Water, rest and flexible timing matter more than extra stops.
JULY–SEPTEMBER

Green & dramatic.

Best for cloud landscapes, waterfalls and monsoon atmosphere. Mandu and Pachmarhi are strong choices. Rain can change roads, viewpoints, waterfall safety and forest access.

ManduPachmarhiJabalpurOrchha
Wildlife note: core safari and buffer access are season- and reserve-specific. Check the concerned forest authority; never assume an old safari plan is open.
FESTIVALS & LONG WEEKENDS

Plan for crowds.

Temples, popular city attractions and hotel areas can be much busier around major holidays and local events. Book stay early, use morning slots and keep a backup route.

UjjainOmkareshwarMaheshwarTravel essentials
Before booking: confirm temple/safari timing, road route, hotel cancellation policy and whether the day has a weekly or festival closure.
