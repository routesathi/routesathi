Seasonal guide

When should you visit Madhya Pradesh?

Choose the destination with the weather in mind. This simple guide helps you avoid an uncomfortable plan before you book tickets.

Quick rule: October to February is best for heritage cities and comfortable sightseeing. Summer suits hill stations and safaris if you plan early mornings. Monsoon makes waterfalls, forests and Mandu especially beautiful, but outdoor access can change.

OCTOBER – FEBRUARY

Comfortable exploring

The most comfortable period for full-day heritage walks, temples, forts, lakes and food trails. It is also the busiest season around Christmas, New Year and long weekends.

GwaliorFort, palaces and heritage lanes in pleasant weather. KhajurahoTemple exploration is easiest in cooler mornings and evenings. OrchhaRiverside calm, cenotaphs and heritage walks. UjjainTemple visits and old-city walks without summer heat. BhopalLakes, museums and nearby heritage stops.

Plan smart: Book stays and key transport early for festival dates and long weekends.

MARCH – JUNE

Heat-smart travel

Summer can be very hot across the plains. Choose a hill station, plan outdoor stops at sunrise, rest in the afternoon, and keep an unhurried evening plan.

PachmarhiMP’s hill station; a better summer choice than the hot plains. KanhaMorning and afternoon safari slots; follow the forest booking rules. BandhavgarhWildlife trip with early starts and a cool-down break. PenchPlan around safari timings, heat and your gate location.

Plan smart: Never keep a long outdoor fort or temple route in the middle of the day.

JULY – SEPTEMBER

Green & dramatic

This is the green season—excellent for waterfalls, hills and rain-washed heritage landscapes. Keep flexible time because rain, road conditions and access rules can change.

ManduRain turns Mandu’s plateau, lakes and monuments dramatic. PachmarhiWaterfalls and forest views are at their greenest. JabalpurWaterfall and marble-rock plans need weather awareness. OrchhaA quieter heritage break; carry rain protection.

Plan smart: Check forest, waterfall, boating and road status locally before leaving your hotel.

FESTIVALS & LONG WEEKENDS

Plan for crowds

Some destinations are best experienced with extra preparation: earlier darshan, a stay close to your first stop, and a little buffer in your route.

UjjainMahakal and major festival dates can mean heavy crowds. OmkareshwarTemple town routes benefit from an early start. MaheshwarBook riverside stays earlier during popular dates. MP festivals & eventsSee crowd, stay and route guidance before choosing a festival date.

Before you book

Weather, opening days, safari gates, boat rides and road conditions can change. Verify the current official update before you leave, especially during monsoon and festival periods.
